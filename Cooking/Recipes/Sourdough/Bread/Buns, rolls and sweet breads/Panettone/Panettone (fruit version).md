## Directions:

It is assumed that the LM is maintained using the cloth method ([[Lievito madre]]) at 42-50% hydration. I feed at 1:2 ratio every day at 09:30-10:00 a.m. (Zernari - 13.5g protein).
### Refreshments (FR-ST):
Need recalculation? [[Recalculation of 3 feedings|Run this script]] to decide on initial inoculation of LM
1. *FR: 09:30*: 10g LM : 8g water : 20g white Panettone flour
2. **If the maintenance is messed up** and/or there are suspicions about LM, perform [[Lievito madre|Bagnetto]]
	> 300ml of 25-28C water, 1g of sugar, stir with a wooden spoon. Remove excessively hydrated parts of the dough from LM with your finger and tear big pieces into Bagnetto, wait for 20 minutes.
3. *ST 10:00-10:20*: 1st refreshment
4. *ST 14:20-14:40*: 2nd refreshment
5. *ST 18:40-19:00*: 3rd refreshment

- Place the hook into the freezer. The mixing bowl as well if possible, if not - refrigerate.
- Measure and place the water into the fridge.
### First kneading (ST 23:00):
1. Thoroughly by hand, mix <u>the sourdough starter</u> torn into pieces (don't forget to discard the extra) and <u>cold water</u> (NOT all of it) in a bowl of a stand mixer until foamy.
2. Put the <u>flour</u> and <u>sugar</u> into a mixing bowl. Knead as far as it is able to develop. Add more water if it is too stiff slowly.
> Do not forget that we are supposed to give it rests and gather the dough manually unless you use a spiral mixer.
3. Add the <u>butter</u> gradually in a few batches while mixing. Make sure the dough is strong and not slack.
4. Now the dough must look smooth, glossy, detached from the walls, not too dry, not too wet. Add the <u>egg yolks</u> gradually, while mixing. The consistency will be slightly more wet and sticky. It will first start to break down, that's OK. When the yolks are mixed in slightly, we may increase the speed.
5. Take the dough out from the mixer onto the counter. Fold it a few times to form a strong big boule.
6. Wait until it triples in size + slightly more (8-13h) at *25-26C*.

> The more it takes and not accelerated with even higher temperatures (min. 25-26C is crucial for the structure), the more preservation time we have, the more moisture retainment longevity. 8h - 2 weeks, 12-13h - 3 weeks/up to 1 month. It still must be safe to eat even after 3 weeks, but it won't be perfect.

> (Optional) Once the fermentation time has passed, place it in the refrigerator before proceeding to the second kneading. This step will make the dough easier to handle and less prone to overheating.

7. Make the [[Chocolate glaze|Chocolate glaze]] (if not making artisanal version with butter and crisscrossing) and place into the fridge overnight.
8. Wash the dry fruit thoroughly and soak overnight in minimum amount of cold water. Then, in the morning, drain them in the colander and put in the fridge!
> DO NOT soak candied peels, but DO SOAK a handful for flavor, this is very important. 
9. Then, add the chocolate chips and cover all the inclusions with a tablespoon of inverted syrup or oil before adding to the dough.
### Second kneading (SU ~11 a.m.):
At this stage, the perfect pH must be 5.1 (claim those who wait until it quadruples). 
1. Degas the first dough in the hand mixer on low.
2. Add the <u>flour</u> all at once ON LOW (careful not to raise a cloud of it). Aim for strength and structure (4-5 min). This is the most crucial step. All the flour must be incorporated and well-kneaded into the dough without adding any other ingredients at this step. (When the flour is slightly mixed in, increase the speed a bit). In the end, the dough must not stick to the walls (almost at least), be shiny and glossy, pass the window pane test.
3. Add all the <u>sugar</u> with <u>1/3 of egg yolks</u>. The yolks will moisturize the sugar, making it softer and more easily accessible for the dough.
4. When the dough has regained its consistency again, add the <u>inverted sugar / honey</u> with another<u> 1/3 of the egg yolks</u> all at once. This will help to dissolve the inverted sugar. Work the dough until homogeneous again.
5. Add the fine <u>salt</u> with the <u>remaining 1/3 egg yolk</u>. Regain the texture working for a few minutes again.
6. Start adding <u>the butter</u> gradually in 3-4 additions, it must not be too soft, better colder. Recover the dough again.
7. (Optional) Add the <u>water</u> very slowly, time to time. Let it be fully absorbed by the dough.
8. Now, choose what panettone to make: fruit / chocolate / pistachio etc.

### Fruit panettone

9. Add <u>vanilla seeds / orange zest / lemon zest</u> freshly grated. Mix 2 to 3 min.
10. Add the <u>candied orange and lemon with raisins, cranberries and chocolate chips</u> all at once. The fruit and chocolate chips must be moist. Either soaked and drained, or oiled/in syrup. Mix 2 to 3 minutes.
11. Take the dough out of the machine and rest it for an hour or half on a flat OILED surface, uncovered at <u>ROOM TEMPERATURE</u>
12. Divide into portions folding each piece once with one hand while dividing the rest. Each mold must contain [[How much dough goes into the mold | about 1/3 grams of its volume]].  For biggest ones use 1000g.
13. Make three folds for each dough piece and then round with a scraper. Set aside for 30 min.
14. Meanwhile, grab the metal turning needles for panettone and pierce the molds. The dough is going to ferment and develop around the needles. Prepare the resting station now.
15. Round the doughs, place into molds (always keep the upper part of the dough on top. It is less sticky).
16. If the dough is at 26C and the environment too, then it is 6-8 hour proof having covered each with a plastic wrap. 

> Each degree below will make us wait 1 hour more. Each degree above may melt the butter too much. It is ready to be baked when the dough is almost near the edges of the molds (about 1.5-2cm below the rim).
### Baking

16. Apply the glaze on top, but not the edges, just wait until it's spread there. *Be EXTREMELY moderate with the glaze! It gets on the way of the oven spring and its heaviness may cause the dough to collapse*
17. Top the <u>glaze</u> with <u>coarse sugar</u>.
18. Insert a probe of a thermometer into the top left panettone on the tray at a VERY SLIGHT angle to the center, not 180, but about 200degrees so that when the dough expands the probe ends up in its geometrical center.
19. Bake 2 at a time (if 1kg): 5min at 200C no fan, then 50 minutes 160C NO FAN until it's 95-96C inside. No need to rotate the tray. 
20. Make sure the resting station is prepared.
21. Hang them upside down overnight (the chef said it's fine to just wait until they have cooled to 28C). Do it fast before they collapse.
