## Directions:

It is assumed that the LM is maintained using the cloth method ([[Lievito madre]]) at 42-50% hydration. I feed at 1:2 ratio every day at 09:30-10:00 a.m. (Zernari - 13.3g protein).
### Refreshments (FR-ST):
Need recalculation? [[Recalculation of 3 feedings|Run this script]] to decide on initial inoculation of LM
1. *FR: 09:30*: 10g LM : 8g water : 20g white Panettone flour
2. **If the maintenance is messed up** and/or there are suspicions about LM, perform [[Lievito madre|Bagnetto]]
	> 300ml of 25-28C water, 1g of sugar, stir with a wooden spoon. Remove excessively hydrated parts of the dough from LM with your finger and tear <u>big</u> pieces into Bagnetto, wait for 20 minutes.
3. *ST 10:00-10:20*: 1st refreshment
4. *ST 14:20-14:40*: 2nd refreshment
5. *ST 18:40-19:00*: 3rd refreshment

- Place the hook into the freezer. The mixing bowl as well if possible, if not - refrigerate.
- Measure and place the water into the fridge.
### First kneading (ST 23:00):
1. Thoroughly by hand, in a small glass bowl, mix <u>the sourdough starter</u> torn into pieces (don't forget to discard the extra) and <u>cold water</u> (NOT all of it) until a foamy slurry forms.
> slower the fermentation for the time mixing; dispersing chunks of dough evenly; not to "preheat" the mixer trying to mix them there + it won't handle such small amount
2. Put the <u>LM slurry</u> and the <u>flour</u> into a mixing bowl. Knead to fully develop. Add more water if it is too stiff, slowly.
3. Meanwhile, in the same small glass bowl, add all the <u>sugar</u> and thoroughly mix it with the <u>egg yolks</u> aiming to dissolve the sugar.
> Do not forget that we are supposed to give it rests and gather the dough manually unless you use a spiral mixer.
4. After the dough:
	- isn't shaggy
	- has completely cleared the sides of the bowl
	- shows strong resistance and can stretch significantly, but might still tear with a jagged edge (NO WINDOWPANE)
	slowly add the <u>egg-sugar mixture</u> in a few goes.
> DO NOT let it go as far as windowpane, the gluten network will be tightly locked and fight the liquid. This will result in destroying gluten bonds that you built + additional friction heat.
5. Add the <u>remaining water</u> if necessary. Achieve windowpane!
6. Add the <u>butter</u> gradually in a few batches.
7. Now the dough must look smooth, glossy, detached from the walls, not too dry, not too wet. Add the <u>egg yolks</u> gradually, while mixing. The consistency will be slightly more wet and sticky. It will first start to break down, that's OK. When the yolks are mixed in slightly, we may increase the speed.
8. Take the dough out from the mixer onto the counter. Fold it a few times to form a strong big boule.
9. Wait until it triples in size + slightly more (8-13h) at *25-26C*.

> The more it takes and not accelerated with even higher temperatures (min. 25-26C is crucial for the structure), the more preservation time we have, the more moisture retainment longevity. 8h - 2 weeks, 12-13h - 3 weeks/up to 1 month. It still must be safe to eat even after 3 weeks, but it won't be perfect.

> (Optional) Once the fermentation time has passed, place it in the refrigerator before proceeding to the second kneading. This step will make the dough easier to handle and less prone to overheating.

10. Make the [[Chocolate glaze|Chocolate glaze]] (if not making artisanal version with butter and crisscrossing) and place into the fridge overnight.
11. Wash the dry fruit thoroughly and soak overnight in minimum amount of cold water. Then, in the morning, drain them in the colander and put in the fridge!
> DO NOT soak candied peels, but DO SOAK a handful for flavor, this is very important. 
12. Then, add the chocolate chips and cover all the inclusions with a tablespoon of inverted syrup or oil before adding to the dough.
