## Directions:


### ----- Day 1 -----:

#### Biga

1. Dissolve the yeast in warm milk (you can rub it with a fork). Mix with the flour until no dry spots remain (it will be a shaggy, stiff ball). This is called Biga. Make it look like a round ball as much as possible.
2. **First**, leave it at room temperature for 10-15min. **Then**, into the fridge in a closed container in the fridge. As it will be rising, you will see a round dome in the center. The best timing to use it is when that dome becomes slightly flat, less round (about **20-24h**).

#### Dry fruit:
1.  Soak the dry fruit in cold water overnight or a day before. Some people add some rum or freshly-squeezed orange juice also. I usually use a splash of rum + cold water for 70g lemon-orange peels, 111g raisins (ensure no preservatives). The water should be a few centimeters above the fruit to submerge them fully.

### ----- Day 2 -----:

#### Main dough kneading

1. Take **Biga** out of the fridge 30-60min before working with it (optional; it’s just cold to the touch).

≻ As we are doing it with no stand-mixer, we will need to work in intervals. The first step is called autolyze. It lets the flour hydrate and strengthen (like passive kneading). We don’t add sugar, far or any other gluten-harming substances at this stage.
2. Main dough section: mix the **flour**, **milk**, and all **eggs** + **yolks** into a shaggy mass. Cover and rest for 45 minutes.

≻ Since it will be sticky, you would want to use a technique called **Slap and Fold (also known as the Bertinet Method).** It is highly advisable that you google it. Simple and efficient.
3. Tear the **Biga** into small pieces and knead them into the autolyze dough. Slap and Fold. Repeat for 10–15 minutes until the dough is smooth and stops sticking to the table.

≻ Because adding sugar and butter by hand can "melt" the dough into a soup, we use the **Rubaud Method** (scooping and lifting the dough with one hand like a whisk). Please google it as well. **HOWEVER**, if you have a mixer, they often come with hook attachments – use it for the next steps, much easier!
4. **Mix sugar and honey**, let it sit for a few minutes until becomes syrupy. Add the **sugar/honey** syrup in 4 stages. Knead until the dough "re-tightens" after each addition.
5. Rub **the softened butter** into the dough last. It will feel like it’s falling apart—keep going; it will eventually emulsify. Add **the salt** with the last bit of butter.
6. Add the dry fruit.

#### Bulk ferment

≻ The first rise of the dough in warm conditions is called “bulk ferment”.
1. Bulk ferment the dough at **24-26C** for 2-3h or until doubled. Try not to exceed these temperatures. And don’t forget to cover the dough!

#### Final proof
1. Divide the dough in 3 pcs. Shape them into rounds and put into molds **(~420g** of dough for each mold: 13cm in diameter, 8.5cm height). Proof at **26C** again until they are 2cm from the rim.

#### Baking
1. Preheat the oven to 180C (**but we'll be baking at 160C!**)
2. Brush each kulich top with milk generously.
3. Put in the oven (middle level), turn the fan off and immediately drop the temperature to 160C. Bake for ~35min. The tops must be golden brown, but the most reliable factor is a thermometer showing **94-96C** in the middle.
4. Cool on a rack to allow the steam to escape from the bottom (запобігає ефекту гливкуватості)
