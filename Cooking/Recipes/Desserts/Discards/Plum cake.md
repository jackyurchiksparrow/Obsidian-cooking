> [!important]
> do not attempt to make this cake with low-moisture and/or acid fruit. Choose other citrus recipes for that. This results in cracked and drier cake. Reasoning:
> 1. acid build up and evaporation tighten the pores on top
> 2. less liquid in fruit, easier to set the outer surface earlier
> 
> If you still want to proceed, you would need to increase liquid in batter (milk) and cover the pan in the very beginning of baking at 160C, then uncover and finish at 180C.

## Directions:


1. Get the butter out in advance, soak the discard in advance.
2. Grease a 26 cm springform cake pan. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon. If using small (3 inches wide or smaller) plums, halve them; if larger, cut into quarters.
3. In a large bowl, use an electric mixer on medium-high speed to cream the butter and sugar for 3 minutes, scraping down the bowl every minute or so. Beat in starter, egg, vanilla on medium speed for 1-2 minutes. Beat in the dry ingredients on low speed until just combined.
4. Scrape the batter into the prepared pan. Top with the plums, skin-side up. Sprinkle with more granulated or Demerara sugar.
5. You may optionally put the batter into the fridge overnight.
6. Bake for 40-50 minutes at 180C, until the cake is deeply golden, starts to pull away from the edges of the pan, and a cake tester inserted into the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs (err on the higher side for smaller pans, lower side for bigger pans). As soon as the cake comes out of the oven, sprinkle the surface with flaky salt. Let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then release the outer spring and let cool for at least another 30 minutes, but ideally completely, before removing from the pan and slicing.
7. To prevent the plums from disappearing into the batter, add half at the start of baking, and the other way halfway through the bake time (or just put it upside down).
